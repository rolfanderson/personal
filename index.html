Description

This project, currently nameless, develops a central theory of space, upon which it emphasizes particular places demonstrative of these principles. Rhetoric and composition dwells at what Diane Davis calls "the spatial turn." Therefore this thesis first takes into account some of the field's recent inquiries into writing in space. Place, however, reminds us of the importance of material particulars.
